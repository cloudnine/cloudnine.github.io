Author: Yuri Tricys |   Feb 4, 2020  |    learning center, non fiction
This is a fantastic book. I bought it when it was first published (in 2002), and my copy is well used. The idea seems to be basically to publish everything you need to know about business, that a book can teach you, between two covers. The reality of course is that there are a lot of books on business, and many of them are inspiring and educational. Moreover, books like this one are great for people who want to understand key processes in big business, and even helpful for those who want to build better small business, but, despite what Chekhov said about it, business is not about processes, buzz words, or other people's money, it's about value exchange.

Books are Still a Thing

Don't listen to what anybody says, books are still useful. You can stand on them, use them as coasters. They're cheap, so they make great Christmas gifts, and they look great on your shelves. Even more seriously, there's no better gadget in a bathtub.

Okay, granted it's the twenty-first century, we're busy, and we have tech. You can't spend all day reading, but you should read books everyday. There's still no better way smarten-up, and let's face it, they smell great and they're easy on the eyes.

Now if you're about to head on down to your local bookstore and buy the latest dictionary, don't forget to browse here first. Every now and then we need to write something up and there is nothing sweeter than recollecting well-read treasures.

"I try to make time for reading each night. In addition to the usual newspapers and magazines, I make it a priority to read at least one newsweekly from cover to cover. If I were to read what intrigues me- say, the science and business sections - then I would finish the magazine the same person I was when I started. So I read it all." ~ Bill Gaites
