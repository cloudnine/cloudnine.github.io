Algis Budrys, was a wonderful Science Fiction author from back in the day, when men wanted to land on the moon and science fiction was the latest greatest thing. When I was a teenager, I found a story of his in a magazine my father had been reading. His name was circled in pen. My father didn't tell war stories, or talk about being Lithuanian, but I found signs of Lithuanianism everywhere - and my dad had a thing for science fiction. That's how I got my name ðŸ˜Ž

One of the stories I read was called 'Citadel.' When I first read it, I didn't get the cold war references, or the fact that Algis, an Honary Consulate of Lithuania, had painted a rather allegorical picture of international relations. In 'Citadel' men have expanded into the universe and encountered aliens. We are the bad guys. Our system profligates from and prompts by-and-large economic invasion of alien planets. Writing from a position in North America (I think Budrys lived in New York then), the Soviets could be the aliens in the story and Americans the humans. So the story is repeat of that old Marxist yarn that capitalism can't work out in the end. Personally, I couldn't agree more - capitalism is broken, just like every other system that's been tried.

Have you ever asked yourself why there was a market for the bible, or why self-help books and motivational speakers are always publishing and always in demand? It's not the system that's broken.

Anyway, you can read 'Citadel' in twenty minutes. Here it is.

Books are Still a Thing

Don't listen to what anybody says, books are still useful. You can stand on them, use them as coasters. They're cheap, so they make great Christmas gifts, and they look great on your shelves. Even more seriously, there's no better gadget in a bathtub.

Okay, granted it's the twenty-first century, we're busy, and we have tech. You can't spend all day reading, but you should read books everyday. There's still no better way smarten-up, and let's face it, they smell great and they're easy on the eyes.

Now if you're about to head on down to your local bookstore and buy the latest dictionary, don't forget to browse here first. Every now and then we need to write something up and there is nothing sweeter than recollecting well-read treasures.

"I try to make time for reading each night. In addition to the usual newspapers and magazines, I make it a priority to read at least one newsweekly from cover to cover. If I were to read what intrigues me- say, the science and business sections - then I would finish the magazine the same person I was when I started. So I read it all." ~ Bill Gaites
